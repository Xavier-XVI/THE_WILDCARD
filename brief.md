# Client Brief — COMP 8: THE WILDCARD
**Submitted by:** Xavier Vincent  
**Date:** June 2026

---

## The Problem

I am a remote AI consultant. My work begins with discovery — understanding how a client's business actually runs before designing any automation or AI solution.

The bottleneck: discovery requires synchronous time. Video calls. Scheduled sessions. Back-and-forth to fill gaps. When my client is a busy solopreneur in a different timezone, this becomes the slowest part of the entire engagement. Hours are lost to scheduling, not to building.

My client Colin Thompson runs Oligye Enterprises, a life coaching business offering career coaching, personal development, executive coaching, and relocation support. His process lives in three places: partial documentation, scattered recordings, and his own head. Extracting it requires a skilled interviewer who knows how coaching businesses work — someone who can ask the right follow-up, spot the gap, and push past surface-level answers.

I don't always have that person available. And Colin doesn't always have the time.

## What I Built

A folder-based discovery agent that runs the interview for me.

It arrives pre-loaded with Colin's public business profile and deep knowledge of how life coaching businesses operate — so it never asks what's already known. It conducts structured async discovery across five rounds, accepts files and documents Colin shares along the way, and when complete, walks Colin through a visual review using SVG workflow diagrams to confirm accuracy.

The output is a two-format spec document: human-readable for my review, builder-ready for handoff to an AI agent.

## The Goal

Reduce discovery from 3–5 hours of scheduled calls to a single 30-minute review — on the client's schedule, not mine. This is not a replacement for human judgment. It's the infrastructure that makes human judgment faster and better-targeted.
