# Reference — Standard Lead Journey Map for Life Coaches

> This is the typical path from "never heard of this coach" to "signed paying client" in a solo life coaching practice. Use this as your baseline hypothesis entering the session. Ask Colin to confirm, correct, or fill gaps at each stage — do not assume his journey matches this exactly.

---

## The Six Stages

### Stage 1 — Awareness
How a potential client first becomes aware the coach exists.

**Common channels (ask which apply):**
- Referral from a past or current client
- LinkedIn post or article
- Organic Google search (website or content)
- Speaking engagement or podcast appearance
- Instagram or Facebook content
- Personal network (friend, colleague mentions the coach)
- Corporate program (company brings in the coach for a team)

**What to ask:**
- Which of these is the primary source of new inquiries?
- Has that changed in the last year?
- Is there a channel he's invested in that isn't producing?

---

### Stage 2 — First Contact
The moment the potential client reaches out.

**Common forms:**
- Contact form on website
- Direct email
- DM on LinkedIn, Instagram, or Telegram
- Phone call
- WhatsApp or Telegram message
- Introduction from a mutual connection (warm referral)

**What to ask:**
- What does first contact most commonly look like for Colin?
- How quickly does he typically respond?
- Is there a standard first response, or does he write each one fresh?
- What does he say in that first reply?

---

### Stage 3 — Qualification & Scheduling
The gap between first contact and the intro session.

**What typically happens here:**
- Coach replies with a brief message + scheduling link
- Some coaches send a short pre-screening form (what brings you here, what are you hoping for)
- Some coaches have a phone call first to assess fit before offering the full intro session
- Some go straight to booking with no pre-screening

**What to ask:**
- Does Colin pre-screen before booking the intro session?
- Who controls the scheduling? (coach sends link / client books directly / goes back and forth)
- What does the prospect receive after booking? (confirmation email, prep instructions, nothing?)
- How long is the typical gap between first contact and intro session?

---

### Stage 4 — Intro Session
The no-cost, no-commitment first conversation. This is the make-or-break moment.

**What typically happens:**
- Coach learns about the prospect's situation and goals
- Coach explains what coaching is and how they work
- Both parties assess whether there is a fit
- At the end: coach makes a recommendation (coaching is a fit / isn't a fit)
- Prospect decides whether to move forward

**What to ask:**
- Does Colin follow a structure in the intro session, or does it flow naturally?
- How does he transition from "intro conversation" to "do you want to move forward"?
- What does he do at the end of an intro session when it goes well? And when it doesn't?
- How does he follow up after the intro session? How quickly? What does he send?

---

### Stage 5 — Intake & Agreement
What happens when a prospect says yes.

**What typically happens:**
- Coaching Agreement is sent for signature
- Kickoff Workbook or intake assessment is shared
- First paid session is scheduled (sometimes the intake session itself is the first paid session)
- Payment is collected (upfront for a package, or first session payment)

**What to ask:**
- In what order does this happen? (sign first then pay, or pay first then sign?)
- How is the agreement sent? (DocuSign, PDF by email, through a platform?)
- What is the Kickoff Workbook — what does it contain and how is it used?
- How long does it typically take between someone saying yes and their first paid session?

---

### Stage 6 — Ongoing Engagement
The active coaching relationship.

**What typically happens:**
- Regular sessions (weekly or bi-weekly, 60 min)
- Between-session accountability or check-ins (varies widely)
- Progress tracking against focus goals
- Periodic re-alignment on goals as the engagement evolves

**Ending the engagement:**
- Engagement ends at package completion, or naturally when goals are achieved
- Some coaches have a structured close (final session, reflection, next steps)
- Some end informally when sessions stop

**What to ask:**
- What does a typical ongoing session look like from start to finish?
- What does Colin send the client after a session?
- How does he track what each client committed to?
- What between-session communication, if any, is standard?
- How do engagements typically end — is there a structured close?
- Do past clients come back? Is there a re-engagement offer?
- Does Colin ask for referrals? When and how?

---

## The Hidden Stages (Where Things Fall Apart)

These are the gaps between stages that don't get documented but matter most for automation:

**Between Stage 2 and 3:** The follow-up gap. Prospect reaches out → coach doesn't reply quickly enough → prospect books with someone else or loses momentum.

**Between Stage 4 and 5:** The post-intro gap. Great intro session → coach delays the follow-up summary email → prospect's excitement fades → no conversion.

**Between Stage 5 and 6:** The onboarding gap. Client says yes → paperwork and logistics are slow or confusing → client starts with a bad impression before the first real session.

**During Stage 6:** The between-session gap. Client leaves a session energized → hears nothing for two weeks → accountability fades → results are weaker than they could be.

**After Stage 6:** The offboarding gap. Engagement ends → no structured close, no testimonial collected, no alumni touchpoint → past client relationship atrophies.

Ask Colin specifically about each of these. Not "does this happen to you" (he'll say no) — but "walk me through the last time [specific gap scenario] happened."
