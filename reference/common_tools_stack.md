# Reference — Common Tools Stack for Life Coaching Businesses

> Use this as a hypothesis map. Before asking Colin "what tools do you use," you should already know what tools coaches typically use at each stage. Ask Colin to confirm, replace, or mark as "not using."

---

## Tools by Business Function

### Scheduling
Tools coaches use to let clients book sessions without email back-and-forth.

| Tool | Notes |
|---|---|
| Calendly | Most common. Free tier works for basic scheduling. Paid tier adds buffers, team features, payment integration. |
| Acuity Scheduling | Strong for coaches — handles packages, intake forms at booking, payment. |
| TidyCal | Simpler, cheaper alternative to Calendly. |
| CoachAccountable | Coaching-specific platform that includes scheduling + much more. |
| Paperbell | Coaching-specific. Handles scheduling, contracts, payments, client portal in one. |
| Notion + Calendly combo | Some coaches embed Calendly into a Notion client hub. |

**What to confirm with Colin:** Which tool does his schedule link use? Does he have it set up to send automatic confirmations and reminders, or does he manage those manually?

---

### CRM / Contact Management
How coaches track leads, conversations, and client history.

| Tool | Notes |
|---|---|
| Google Sheets | The most common "CRM" for solo coaches — a spreadsheet with lead name, status, last contact. Manual and inconsistent. |
| Notion | Used as a lightweight CRM by many solo operators. Flexible but requires discipline to maintain. |
| HubSpot (free) | Some coaches use the free CRM tier. More structured than Sheets. |
| CoachAccountable | Has a built-in client management system designed for coaches. |
| Paperbell | Also handles client records. |
| Nothing formal | Very common — especially when client volume is low enough to track by memory. |

**What to confirm with Colin:** How does he track who he's in conversation with? Is there a list somewhere, or does he rely on his email inbox and memory?

---

### Contracts & Agreements
How coaching agreements are sent, signed, and stored.

| Tool | Notes |
|---|---|
| DocuSign | Industry standard for e-signatures. Paid. |
| HelloSign (now Dropbox Sign) | Popular alternative to DocuSign. |
| PandaDoc | Handles proposals + contracts in one. |
| PDF by email | Coach sends PDF, client signs and scans or types name. Very common at solo scale. |
| CoachAccountable / Paperbell | Both handle agreements within the platform. |
| Google Docs | Some coaches use a shared Google Doc that clients "sign" by adding their name. |

**What to confirm with Colin:** How does he send the Coaching Agreement? Does he have to manually trigger this or is it automatic after someone books?

---

### Payment
How coaches collect money.

| Tool | Notes |
|---|---|
| Stripe | Most common. Used directly or as the payment processor behind other tools. |
| PayPal | Common, especially for international clients. |
| Square | Less common for coaches but used by some. |
| CoachAccountable / Paperbell | Both have payment processing built in. |
| Bank transfer / wire | Common for international or corporate clients. |
| Invoice by email | Manual invoicing via a tool like Wave (free) or FreshBooks. |

**What to confirm with Colin:** When does payment happen — upfront for a package, per session, or on a monthly cycle? Who initiates — does the client pay via a link, or does Colin send an invoice?

---

### Session Delivery
How coaching sessions actually happen.

| Tool | Notes |
|---|---|
| Zoom | Most common. |
| Google Meet | Free, widely used. |
| Microsoft Teams | More common for corporate/organizational clients. |
| Phone call | Still used by many coaches, especially for clients who prefer it. |
| In person | For local clients. |

**What to confirm with Colin:** What platform does he use for video sessions? Does he record sessions (with consent)? Does he use any session tools (shared screen, whiteboard, etc.)?

---

### Session Notes & Client Tracking
How coaches capture and store what happened in a session.

| Tool | Notes |
|---|---|
| Google Docs | One doc per client, updated after each session. Very common. |
| Notion | Client database with session notes as entries. Popular with more organized coaches. |
| CoachAccountable | Has a built-in session notes and goal-tracking system. |
| Word / Pages | Local documents. Common but not shareable without extra steps. |
| Physical notebook | Still used by some coaches. |
| Nothing formal | Also common — notes kept in email or memory. |

**What to confirm with Colin:** Does he have a standard format for session notes? Are notes stored somewhere that a client could theoretically access? How does he remember what each client committed to between sessions?

---

### Client Communication (Between Sessions)
How coaches stay in contact with clients outside of scheduled sessions.

| Tool | Notes |
|---|---|
| Email | Default. Most coaches use this for session reminders, follow-ups, and check-ins. |
| WhatsApp | Common for international clients and coaches. Informal. |
| Telegram | Similar to WhatsApp. Colin has a public Telegram channel — likely uses it for client communication too. |
| Voxer | Voice messaging app popular in the coaching world. Allows async voice check-ins. |
| CoachAccountable | Has a client messaging portal built in. |
| SMS | Occasional. |

**What to confirm with Colin:** What channel does he use for between-session communication? Is it consistent across clients or does he adapt to each client's preference? Does he initiate contact between sessions or wait for clients to reach out?

---

### Marketing & Lead Generation
How coaches attract attention and generate inquiries.

| Tool | Notes |
|---|---|
| LinkedIn | Organic content, direct outreach, thought leadership. Very effective for coaching. |
| Instagram | Visual content, stories, coaching quotes. More B2C. |
| Email newsletter | ConvertKit and Mailchimp most common. Builds a list over time. |
| Website (SEO) | Passive lead source if content is published. |
| Podcast / YouTube | Long-form content as a lead magnet. |
| Speaking | Corporate events, associations, conferences. |
| Referral network | Often the largest source of clients for established coaches. |

**What to confirm with Colin:** Which of these is his primary lead source right now? Has he tried any that didn't work? Is there anything he's been meaning to do but hasn't?

## Coaching-Specific Platforms (All-in-One)

These platforms handle scheduling, contracts, payment, notes, and client communication in one place. Know about them because they represent a potential consolidation play for Colin.

| Platform | What it does |
|---|---|
| **CoachAccountable** | Full practice management — scheduling, sessions, goals, notes, client accountability, invoicing. Used by ICF-certified coaches. |
| **Paperbell** | Newer, simpler. Scheduling + contracts + payment + client portal. Good for solo coaches. |
| **Practice** | Clean, modern coaching platform. Scheduling, forms, notes, messaging. |
| **Honeybook** | Not coaching-specific but used by many service businesses. Handles leads, proposals, contracts, payment. |
| **Dubsado** | Similar to Honeybook. More customizable but steeper learning curve. |

**What to confirm with Colin:** Is he using any of these, or has he considered them? Understanding why he hasn't consolidated (or why he did) reveals a lot about his tolerance for tooling change.
