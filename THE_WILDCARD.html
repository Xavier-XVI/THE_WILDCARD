The Problem

The Discovery Bottleneck

Every AI consulting engagement begins the same way: a discovery call. Then another. Then a follow-up to fill the gaps. By the time you understand how a client's business actually operates, you've spent three to five hours on video — and most of that time was scheduling, not building.

The problem isn't the conversation. The problem is what the conversation requires: a skilled interviewer who arrives prepared, knows the domain, asks the right follow-up, catches the contradiction, and pushes past the polished answer to the real one.

That person is hard to find, expensive to hire, and often unavailable at the moment you need them. So the consultant does it themselves — or the discovery is shallow, and the automation built from it reflects that.

Your client runs their practice out of three places: partial documentation, a scattered inbox, and their own head. Getting it out requires more than a questionnaire. It requires someone who already knows how these businesses work — who can hear "I usually send a follow-up email" and know to ask when, how often, and what happens when they don't.

Generic intake forms produce surface-level answers. Blank AI assistants ask what the website could answer. Live calls work — but they require timezone alignment, calendar negotiation, and synchrony that busy solopreneurs rarely have.

The bottleneck isn't information. It's structured extraction — and it has been stuck behind a calendar invite for too long.

What The Wildcard Does

The Wildcard runs a structured async discovery interview — arriving pre-loaded with the client's public profile and domain knowledge, conducting five interview rounds, and delivering two output formats ready for review and handoff.

R0
Artifact Collection

Client shares any existing documents — contracts, templates, process notes. The agent reads them and adjusts every subsequent question accordingly. Never asks what the documents already answered.

R1
Business Snapshot

Confirms what is already known from the client's public profile. Catches corrections. Establishes conversational rhythm. Never asks what the website could answer.

R2
Lead Journey

Traces how someone goes from not knowing the client to showing up for a first conversation — using real recent examples, not ideal process descriptions.

R3
Delivery & Intent

Maps how the service is actually delivered and captures the job to be done: what the client's clients are really trying to change, and what success looks like six months later.

R4
Friction & Reality

Surfaces what breaks, what gets avoided, and what the client would automate first if they could. This is where the highest-value automation intelligence lives.

Arrives Prepared

Domain knowledge is pre-loaded before the first message: business model, lead journey map, common tools stack. The agent uses this to ask informed questions and spot gaps a blank AI would miss.

Scenario-Based Questions

"Walk me through the last person who reached out" — not "describe your lead generation process." Real examples produce real answers. Process descriptions produce ideal ones.

Session Continuity

Clients can pause at any point and resume later. The agent tracks state automatically — no re-explaining, no restarting. One command saves everything.

Two Output Formats

A human-readable spec for consultant and client review. A builder-ready spec for direct handoff — structured field-by-field, gaps explicitly flagged, no interpretation required.

The Differentiator

Most discovery tools ask questions. The Wildcard knows what the answers should look like — and notices when they don't. It probes vague answers exactly once, catches contradictions warmly, and follows the thread the client didn't expect to pull. The methodology is explicit, named, and testable — not a black box.
