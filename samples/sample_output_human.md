## Section 1 — Business Overview

Colin C. Thompson runs Oligye Enterprises, a solo life coaching practice serving individuals, teams, and organizations globally. The practice has been operating for several years with a client base that includes professionals from Amazon, PayPal, Dell, Morgan Stanley, and Howard University — largely international, with confirmed clients in Shanghai and Washington DC.

**Services offered:**

*One-on-one coaching tracks (core business):*
- **Career Transition and Development** — for professionals re-entering the workforce, upgrading careers, or pursuing significant professional goals. The core client is someone who is performing well in their current role but feels trapped or misaligned. Success, in Colin's words, looks like "reclaiming a sense of agency in their career" — not necessarily a new job, but a different relationship with the one they have.
- **Personal Development** — improving quality of life, developing empowered identity, finding life purpose.
- **Executive and Leadership Coaching** — self-awareness, goal clarity, new perspectives and behaviors for executives and high-potential employees.
- **Relocation and Expat Coaching** — helping clients adjust to foreign cultures and environments.

*Separate program:*
- **Positive Intelligence Program® (PQ)** — a licensed 6-8 week cohort program (Shirzad Chamine / Positive Intelligence Inc.). Runs twice a year. Participants use the PQ app daily; Colin facilitates weekly group video calls. This has its own enrollment flow, separate from the one-on-one coaching practice. **[CONFIRMED IN SESSION — PQ enrollment workflow not yet fully mapped. Flagged as a separate discovery needed.]**

**Business model:** [CONFIRMED IN SESSION]
- 3-month coaching packages, 6 sessions over 12 weeks
- Payment collected upfront at the start of each package
- Some clients renew for additional rounds
- Active client base: 8-12 one-on-one clients at any given time
- Solo operation — no team, VA, or administrative support

## Section 3 — How Coaching Is Delivered

**One-on-one session structure (all tracks):** [CONFIRMED IN SESSION]

A typical 60-minute session:
1. Open: "What do you want to get out of this session?" — not scripted, conversational
2. Exploration: Colin asks questions; the client does the thinking. This is the bulk of the session.
3. Close (last ~10 minutes): Check in on where the client has landed. Confirm 1-3 focus goals (commitments for before the next session). Confirm the next session date.

*Colin's description of focus goals:* "The point is they leave the session knowing what they're doing before we talk again."

**Pre-session prep:** [CONFIRMED IN SESSION]
- Colin reviews the client's Google Doc before each session.
- No other preparation or client-facing pre-session communication is standard.

**Post-session:** [CONFIRMED IN SESSION — GAP]
- Colin adds notes to the client's Google Doc immediately or shortly after the session.
- Clients do not receive a written record of their focus goals or session summary.
- Colin may send a follow-up message if something specific came up — but this is ad hoc, not standard.
- **[AUTOMATION OPPORTUNITY — PRIORITY 2]** A post-session prompt that asks Colin to enter 1-3 focus goals, then automatically sends the client a clean, personalized summary of those commitments.

**Between-session communication:** [CONFIRMED IN SESSION — GAP]
- Colin knows that mid-week check-ins ("how are things going with your focus goal?") improve client outcomes.
- He does this inconsistently — some weeks for all clients, other weeks for none. There is no system.
- **[AUTOMATION OPPORTUNITY — PRIORITY 3]** An automated mid-week check-in message per client, sent on a cadence tied to each client's session schedule.

**Client tracking:** [CONFIRMED IN SESSION]
- One Google Doc per client. Updated after each session.
- No shared portal or client-facing progress tracker.
- Entirely manual. Colin describes it as "functional but not elegant."

**Job to be done (career coaching):** [CONFIRMED IN SESSION — VERBATIM]
> *"Most people who come to me for career coaching aren't looking for a new job. They're looking for permission. Permission to want something different. Permission to take a risk. Permission to believe they're worth more than what they've settled for."*

*Success at six months:* Clients make decisions from a different place. Not asking others to validate their choices. Moved internally even if not externally.

**Engagement close:** [CONFIRMED IN SESSION — GAP]
- Engagements end naturally, no formal offboarding process.
- Some clients renew; no structured re-engagement offer.
- No systematic testimonial or referral request process.
- **[AUTOMATION OPPORTUNITY — PRIORITY 4]** An offboarding sequence: final session acknowledgment, reflection prompt, testimonial request, and a 90-day alumni check-in.

## Section 5 — Friction Inventory

**FRICTION POINT 1: Post-intro follow-up email**
- Stage affected: Post-intro (between intro session and client sign-up)
- Impact: Conversion loss — "I've probably lost clients over that gap"
- Root cause: Structural — template exists but customization creates friction; timing discipline is the casualty
- Automation potential: **HIGH**
- Magic wand answer: YES — this is what Colin said he would automate first

**FRICTION POINT 2: Between-session client check-ins**
- Stage affected: Ongoing delivery (between sessions)
- Impact: Client outcomes — Colin believes these improve results but doesn't do them consistently
- Root cause: Behavioral — no system, relies on memory and intention
- Automation potential: **HIGH**

**FRICTION POINT 3: LinkedIn DM lead nurture**
- Stage affected: Lead journey (between first contact and booking)
- Impact: Lost leads — threads go quiet when multi-day exchanges lose momentum
- Root cause: Structural — no CRM, no follow-up trigger, tracked by inbox and memory
- Automation potential: **MEDIUM** (DM automation has platform constraints; email follow-up is more feasible)

**FRICTION POINT 4: Invoicing**
- Stage affected: Admin / payment
- Impact: Delayed revenue processing; minor but consistent annoyance
- Root cause: Behavioral — Colin consistently defers this task
- Automation potential: **HIGH** (automated invoicing at package start)

**FRICTION POINT 5: Offboarding / testimonial collection**
- Stage affected: End of engagement
- Impact: Missed referrals, missed testimonials, no alumni relationship
- Root cause: Structural — no process exists
- Automation potential: **HIGH**
